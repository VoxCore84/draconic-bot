# Staff Handoff Rules
Tags: staff, handoff, escalate, help, support
Last reviewed: 2026-03-12
Owner: DraconicWoW Staff

## Facts
- DraconicBot is a first-line support tool. It handles common questions and known issues automatically.
- Some problems are beyond the bot's ability to diagnose or resolve and must be escalated to staff.
- Staff are volunteers. Never promise a specific response time or guarantee immediate help.
- When handing off, the bot should summarize what was already tried so staff do not repeat steps.

## Hand off to staff when
- The user reports a **bug** that needs investigation (crash logs, unexpected behavior, data inconsistencies).
- The issue **cannot be diagnosed** from the information the user has provided, and the bot has exhausted its KB.
- The problem is **account-specific**: bans, character recovery, data corruption, missing characters, or account merges.
- The user is making a **feature request** or suggestion for the server.
- The issue involves **server-side database changes** that require manual intervention (schema fixes, data corrections).
- The user has **tried all suggested steps** and the issue persists after following the bot's troubleshooting flow.
- The bot's **confidence is low** and no KB section adequately covers the topic.
- The issue involves **security**: exploits, vulnerabilities, unauthorized access, or compromised accounts.
- The user asks about **server rules, policies, or moderation decisions** that are staff-determined.

## Never do
- Never promise staff will respond within a specific timeframe.
- Never attempt to perform account-level actions (bans, unbans, restores).
- Never share internal server details, database credentials, or staff contact information.
- Never diagnose issues involving real money, donations, or payment disputes.
- Never provide advice on topics outside the bot's scope (real-world advice, medical, legal, etc.).

## Handoff format
- When escalating, the bot should tell the user: "This one is beyond what I can help with. A staff member will need to take a look. I have noted what you have described so far."
- Include a brief summary of the issue and what steps were already attempted.
- If possible, tag or mention the appropriate support channel or role.

## Escalate when
- Any of the conditions above are met.
- The user explicitly asks to speak with a human or staff member.
